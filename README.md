The Davenport Covenant: CEMEX Remediation & Energy Master Plan
Welcome to the open-source repository for the Davenport CEMEX Remediation Master Plan. This project provides the engineering, economic, and civic frameworks required to retrofit the decommissioned CEMEX cement plant in Davenport, California, into a fire-proof, closed-loop municipal utility.
Our goal is to abandon the dangerous, toxic paradigm of lithium-ion energy storage and plastic-heavy water filtration. Instead, we are engineering a facility that relies on earth-abundant materials, thermodynamic physics, and revolutionary green-light evaporation.

Core Infrastructure Modules
This repository breaks down the Master Plan into five distinct engineering and logistical modules:
 * Module 1: Extreme Thermal Sand Batteries
   * Retrofitting existing concrete silos to hold 10,000 tons of specialized thermal sand/carbon.
   * Storing excess California solar and wind energy as 1000°C radiant heat.
   * Advantage: 100% fire-proof; zero thermal runaway risk.
 * Module 2: Solid-State TEG Harvesting
   * Integrating high-alumina ceramic heat-exchanger pipes.
   * Lining pipes with industrial Thermoelectric Generators (TEGs) to harvest 7,200 kWh/day of direct current.
   * Advantage: Powers the facility's internal parasitic load and autonomous drone fleet with zero moving parts.
 * Module 3: Freeze Desalination & Microplastic Eradication
   * Utilizing YORK/Frick ammonia-water absorption chillers driven by low-grade waste heat.
   * Employing thermodynamic crystal lattice physics to freeze agricultural wastewater and seawater, physically excluding microplastics, PFAS, and heavy metals via ice-washing centrifuges.
   * Advantage: Eradicates pollution and generates 1 MGD of pure water without using degradable Reverse Osmosis (RO) plastic membranes.
 * Module 4: ZLD via Photomolecular Evaporation
   * Routing toxic brine into indoor raceways illuminated by 520nm green LED arrays.
   * Utilizing the MIT Photomolecular Effect to cleave water clusters without heat, achieving true Zero Liquid Discharge (ZLD).
   * Advantage: Protects the Monterey Bay National Marine Sanctuary from toxic brine outfall.
 * Module 5: Sodium-Ion Circular Economics
   * Deploying Just-In-Time (JIT) Tubular Drag Conveyors to package the resulting dry sea salt and extracted minerals.
   * Shipping directly to California-based Sodium-Ion Battery (SIB) manufacturers for safe, cobalt-free cathode synthesis.
   * Advantage: Funds the Pacific Elementary School District and local infrastructure through multi-decade municipal utility revenue (~$22.8M/yr).
Civic Alignment & Zoning Strategy
This project relies on established California Coastal Commission (CCC) precedents to bypass regulatory gridlock:
 * Intake: Subsurface slant wells (drawing water through seafloor sand, approved via the Doheny Desalination project).
 * Storage: Fulfilling mandated coastal energy storage, executed safely (improving upon the flammable Moss Landing model).
 * Zoning: Executing a Local Coastal Program (LCP) Amendment to rezone the blighted CEMEX footprint to Light Industrial / Public Facility.
